Layla had been preparing for the city science fair for almost two months. Every afternoon after school, she walked to her grandfather's garage and worked on a small solar-powered water pump. Her first design leaked badly, so she replaced the weak plastic tube with a stronger one. By the end of the week, the pump could move water from one bucket to another without using a battery. Layla wrote her results in a notebook and packed the model carefully in a cardboard box.

On the morning of the fair, Layla woke up before her alarm and checked the box again. Her mother made breakfast while Layla printed the final copy of her poster. They drove to the exhibition hall and arrived early enough to choose a table near the window. Layla set up the pump, filled the buckets, and taped her poster to the display board. When the judges came by, she explained how sunlight powered the small motor.

The judges asked Layla several questions about cost, materials, and possible uses in villages with limited electricity. Layla answered each question calmly because she had practiced with her grandfather the night before. One judge smiled and wrote a long note on his clipboard. After the presentation, Layla walked outside to call her grandfather and tell him that everything had gone well. He congratulated her and told her to stay near her table until the winners were announced.

Suddenly, Layla was back in the garage two weeks earlier, searching for the missing plastic tube before she had ever tested the pump. She wondered whether she should still go to the science fair, even though the judges had already praised her project that morning. A few minutes later, her mother called from the exhibition hall and asked why Layla had abandoned her table. Layla grabbed the cardboard box, although the pump was still supposed to be sitting on display.

When Layla returned to the hall, the final results were already posted near the entrance. Her name appeared in second place, and several students gathered around to ask how the pump worked. Layla smiled and began explaining the design again, but then realized that the buckets on her table were empty. She refilled them, placed the solar panel under the window, and demonstrated the pump one more time. This time, everyone clapped because the water flowed smoothly from one bucket to the other.
